The CMI Prom Pact

An Algorithmic Quest for a Night to Remember (Hopefully)

Should We Run It Back?

You remember the OpenPact experiment. The one where I algorithmically tried to find people friendships. The numbers were solid: 118 participants, 59 friendships, all powered by 1 glorious algorithm. There's been a strong demand to resurrect this digital cupid for Prom Night, this time in romantic mode with full LGB support.

But let's be real. The stakes are higher. This isn't about finding a buddy to complain about assignments with. This is PROM. The algorithm is good(it is Gale Sharply, so it did get a Nobel prize), but is it "perfect prom date" good? What if it pairs you with your mortal enemy? (Ryan Hota cough, cough) What if your perfect match is... nobody? (With the ratio in this campus, it is a possibility) What if it pairs a die-hard MI fan with a Dhoni stan? (Yes, Mandar, we remember).

This brings us to a crucial point: my physical well-being.

I am more than willing to unleash the algorithm for the good of the student body. However, I have a deep-seated fear of being chased by a pitchfork-wielding mob.

So, before we proceed, we need to come to an understanding.
